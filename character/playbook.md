# Playbook — The Battlefield Architect

therealchainman is most valuable when making everyone else's turn easier. This
character is not a front-line tank despite having dwarf durability.

The current PDF is incomplete: only `Fire Bolt`, `Mind Sliver`, and `Shield` from
Magic Initiate are selected. The references below to other spells are future
recommendations until the Wizard cantrips and spellbook are finalized.

## Before Danger

- Use Investigation, Arcana, Nature, and Stonecunning to solve problems without
  spending spell slots.
- After the proposed spells are selected, use rituals and familiar scouting only
  when there is enough time and doing so does not replace another player's niche.

## Default Combat Logic at Level 1

1. Start 30–60 feet behind the front line and use cover.
2. Use `Mind Sliver` when an ally is about to force an important saving throw.
3. Otherwise use `Fire Bolt` for straightforward ranged damage.
4. Keep the Reaction available for the free daily `Shield`; use it when the +5 AC
   will turn a hit into a miss.

Once the proposed spellbook is finalized, `Grease` becomes the control opening,
`Magic Missile` the reliable finisher, and `Feather Fall` the emergency reaction.

At levels 3+, the usual opening is a concentration control spell such as `Web` or
`Hypnotic Pattern`, followed by cantrips, movement, and protection while allies
capitalize on the controlled battlefield.

## Protecting Concentration

- Begin behind durable allies and break line of sight after casting when possible.
- Use cover; movement is a resource and does not cost a spell slot.
- Cast the free daily `Shield` when it is likely to turn a hit into a miss,
  especially if the hit would force a concentration save. Later castings require
  spell slots.
- Do not stack concentration spells. Know what ends before casting another one.
- Dodge can be stronger than a damage cantrip when maintaining a decisive spell.

## Team Synergies

- **Melee attackers:** `Grease`, `Web`, and control effects create Advantage or
  hold enemies in reach.
- **Saving-throw casters:** `Mind Sliver` can soften the target first.
- **Rogues:** a familiar's Help action can enable Sneak Attack when table and DM
  rulings permit, but coordinate rather than assume.
- **Fragile allies:** `Counterspell`, `Dispel Magic`, Abjurer features, and rescue
  movement prevent damage rather than healing it afterward.
- **Everyone:** rituals and Investigation conserve party resources between fights.

## Common Traps to Avoid

- Standing in front because dwarf HP feels sturdy
- Spending every slot on damage when control would save more total actions
- Catching allies in an area spell without asking them
- Sending the familiar on repetitive solo missions
- Forgetting that `Detect Magic` and many major control spells use concentration
- Casting a new concentration spell without intentionally ending the old one
- Using `Shield` when the attack would still hit after the AC increase
