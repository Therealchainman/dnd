# Leveling Plan

This is a straight-class Wizard plan. Multiclassing delays the spells that make a
Wizard an outstanding teammate and is not recommended without a story reason.

The level-4 and later ability values assume the proposed Sage increases of +2
Intelligence and +1 Constitution are applied first. Recalculate this plan if the
builder's current 15 INT and 15 CON already include the background increases.

| Level | Choice | Why it helps |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| 1 | Wizard; Magic Initiate `Fire Bolt`, `Mind Sliver`, and `Shield` | Reliable offense, team setup, and a free emergency defense |
| 2 | Scholar: Investigation Expertise | Makes exploration contribution reliable |
| 3 | Abjurer | Adds a protective identity and team defense |
| 4 | War Caster; INT 17 → 18 | Improves concentration, casting flexibility, DC, and attacks |
| 5 | Third-level control and counters | Major power jump: `Counterspell` and `Hypnotic Pattern` |
| 8 | Ability Score Improvement; INT 18 → 20 | Maximizes core spell accuracy and preparation |
| 12 | Ability Score Improvement; CON 16 → 18 | More HP and stronger concentration checks |

## Why Abjurer

Abjurer matches a sturdy dwarf and gives the build a visible job beyond “cast the
biggest spell”: keep dangerous magic and damage away from the group. It rewards
using spells therealchainman already wants, such as `Shield`, `Counterspell`, and
`Dispel Magic`.

If the table prefers simpler, louder play, Evoker is the fallback: it is easier to
run and lets the Wizard use large damage areas around allies more safely. Record a
subclass change in `memory/build-decisions.md` once the player chooses.

## Party-Based Adjustments

- If nobody can scout, prioritize familiar utility, `Invisibility`, and divination.
- If the party lacks crowd control, prioritize `Web`, `Hypnotic Pattern`, and wall
  spells.
- If the party has several melee characters, favor effects that restrain, knock
  Prone, or divide enemies.
- If another full caster already controls well, prepare more counters, rituals,
  rescue movement, and targeted damage.
- If the campaign is intrigue-heavy, move illusion, language, and enchantment
  spells earlier.

Do not finalize a level-up until the current party and newly found spells have
been reviewed.
